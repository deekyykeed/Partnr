### **The Core Premise**
The primary reason you aren't making as much money as you want is that you are selling to people who don't have money, and you are charging based on what *you* can afford, not what the market can pay. To make big money, you must align your pricing with how wealth is actually distributed.

---

### **Key Takeaways & Frameworks**

**1. The Wealth Distribution Reality**
* Wealth is not distributed evenly. The top 10% of people hold 69% of the wealth. The top 1% has more wealth than the bottom 90% combined.
* Most beginner business owners compete brutally for the bottom 50% of the market (who hold roughly 2% of the total wealth) because that is who they interact with daily. 
* **The Fix:** Go where the money is. Stop selling out of your own wallet and assuming everyone is broke. 

**2. The 80/20 Rule Applied to Profit (Pareto's Principle)**
* 20% of your customers will generate 80% of your profit.
* It goes deeper: Within that top 20%, 4% of your total customers generate 64% of the profit. 
* At the very peak, **1% of your customers generate 51% of your total profit.** * A single person paying $10,000 for something that costs you $2,000 to fulfill is the exact same profit as 400 people buying a $50 thing that costs you $25 to fulfill—but managing one person is infinitely easier than managing 400.

**3. The Tiered Pricing Strategy (The 5–10x Rule)**
When creating an upsell or a higher tier of service, do not make small incremental jumps (e.g., from $100 to $129). The willingness to pay for a premium buyer is massively different than a standard buyer.
* **The Rule:** For every new pricing tier, multiply the price by **5x to 10x**. 
* **The Expectation:** Expect only **20%** of your previous tier's audience to buy the next tier up.
* Even with a 90% "No" rate, the absolute profit generated by the few who say "Yes" will transform your business.

**4. Top-Down Business Building**
* It is easier to start by selling a highly expensive, unscalable service to a few rich people than to build a cheap, automated service for the masses. 
* Serving the masses requires massive volume, razor-thin margins, and millions of dollars in capital to build the infrastructure (e.g., Netflix or Spotify). 
* Anchor your brand high (like Tesla starting with the Roadster before the Model 3). It gives you higher margins, less demanding clients (as a percentage of their net worth, your fee is tiny), and stronger brand positioning.

---

### **Action Steps**
* **Raise Your Upsell Price:** Take your current premium offering and 10x the price. Figure out what insane level of value you would have to provide to justify it, and sell that.
* **Embrace the "No":** Stop optimizing for the most "Yeses." Optimize for the most money. The sweet spot of profitability comes with hearing a lot of nos from unqualified buyers.
* **Break Your Belief Barrier:** You have to ask for a number that scares you. Until you say a massive number out loud and hear a client casually say "done," you will stay trapped in low-ticket pricing.
