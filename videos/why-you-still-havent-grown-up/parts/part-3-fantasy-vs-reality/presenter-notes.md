# Presenter Notes — Part 3: Why Reality Always Disappoints

---

**SLIDE 1 — Title**
- Recap: we know who the Puer is, what they look like
- Today: the core mechanism — why engaging with real life always lets them down
- This is where people start to really recognise themselves

---

**SLIDE 2 — The Core Problem**
- The Puer isn't lacking intelligence or ambition — they have enormous amounts of both
- The problem: every single time they step into real life, reality disappoints
- Not occasionally. Every time. Consistently.
- This isn't pessimism — it's a structural feature of how they experience the world

---

**SLIDE 3 — The Gap**
- The fantasy version of an experience is always better than the lived version
- In their head: vivid, exciting, meaningful
- In reality: grinding, drab, full of friction
- This gap is the engine of everything that follows

---

**SLIDE 4 — This Happens Everywhere**
- Give examples from the script: the novel, the video game, the career
- The idea of building something is incredible. The actual process is tedious.
- *"The version they encounter in real life is a shadow of the one in their head"*
- This is why they start things with enormous energy and abandon them — the real thing never matched the idea

---

**SLIDE 5 — It Happens in Relationships Too**
- Von Franz's quote: searching for the perfect woman (or man) — a mother goddess
- Each time fascinated by someone → discovers they're an ordinary human being → fascination vanishes → moves on → projects the image onto the next person
- Important reframe: *it's not women on a pedestal. It's LIFE on a pedestal.*
- This is bigger than relationships — it's their entire relationship with reality
- Stress this — it's a common misread of the complex

---

**SLIDE 6 — The Cycle**
- Vision → engage → disappointment → retreat to fantasy
- Every time. Reset. Repeat.
- The fantasy is always safer — you can't be disappointed by something you haven't tried
- Close: *"This cycle is the mechanism. Next video: why they can't just choose to break it."*
- Tease Part 4: the pluripotent stem cell — the psychological reason commitment is impossible
