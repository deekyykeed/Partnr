# Presenter Notes — Part 2: What Does the Puer Actually Look Like?

---

**SLIDE 1 — Title**
- Quick recap: last video introduced Puer Eternus — Jung's archetype of the eternal child
- Today: what does this actually look like in a person?
- Audience should be recognising someone they know — or themselves — by the end

---

**SLIDE 2 — First Impression: Impressive**
- Paint the picture clearly: charming, funny, the life of the group
- When you first meet a Puer — you're genuinely impressed
- They're quick. Witty. Easy to like.
- *"People meet them and think: this person is going places"*
- This is important — they're not losers on the surface. They look like winners.

---

**SLIDE 3 — Second Impression: Frustrating**
- But then time passes. And the pattern emerges.
- They start strong — great opener, can't close
- Follow-through? Falls flat. Consistently.
- The potential is real — everyone can see it — but it never goes anywhere
- *"From impressed to frustrated"* — that's the arc people experience with a Puer

---

**SLIDE 4 — The Sleepy Haze**
- Von Franz describes this perfectly: the sleepy, undisciplined youth who merely hangs around
- On the outside: appears unfocused, drifting, lazy
- Makes you want to shake them
- But — and this is key — underneath that haze is an incredibly vivid inner life
- The outer world is quiet. The inner world is going a million miles an hour.

---

**SLIDE 5 — What They're Actually Like Inside**
- They hate small talk because it feels beneath them
- They want to talk about big ideas, deep questions, grand concepts
- *"I want to understand quantum mechanics — I don't want to talk about the weather"*
- The inner world is rich and alive — that's where the potential lives
- The gap between the inner world and the outer world is the whole problem

---

**SLIDE 6 — The Pattern**
- Bring it together: big talker, doesn't show up, potential everyone sees, nobody can explain
- This isn't laziness. It isn't stupidity. It isn't even lack of ambition.
- It's something more specific — and that's what the next video is about
- Tease Part 3: the fantasy vs. reality cycle — why engaging with real life always disappoints them
