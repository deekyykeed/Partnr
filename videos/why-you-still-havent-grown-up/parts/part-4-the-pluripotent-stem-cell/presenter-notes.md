# Presenter Notes — Part 4: Why They Can't Commit

---

**SLIDE 1 — Title**
- Recap: we know the fantasy/reality cycle — reality always disappoints, they retreat
- Question this video answers: why can't they just choose to push through?
- Answer: it's not a choice issue. It's a deeper psychological block.

---

**SLIDE 2 — What They Fear Most**
- Not failure. Not rejection. Those are normal fears.
- What the Puer fears above everything: the loss of potential
- This is the key insight — sit with it before moving on

---

**SLIDE 3 — The Pluripotent Stem Cell**
- This is the best metaphor in the whole series — take your time
- A pluripotent stem cell can become anything: neuron, heart cell, liver cell, pancreas cell
- The moment it differentiates — commits to becoming one thing — it loses the potential to be everything else
- *That is exactly what the Puer is experiencing psychologically*
- They are swimming in potential. And they cannot bear to give it up.

---

**SLIDE 4 — Commitment = Loss**
- Walk through the examples: major, partner, career, city
- Every commitment closes off other options — that's just how choices work
- For most people, that's acceptable. For the Puer, it is pathologically unbearable.
- *"Not won't. Cannot."* — land this distinction
- The fear isn't irrational to them — it's visceral and real

---

**SLIDE 5 — The Provisional Life**
- Read the quote slowly — it's from Von Franz's colleague Baynes, written decades ago
- *"Not yet in real life. For the time being. There's always a fantasy that the real thing will come."*
- This is the lived experience of the Puer: permanent temporariness
- Everything feels like a placeholder. Real life is always just around the corner.

---

**SLIDE 6 — The Boarding Area**
- Airport metaphor — make it visual
- 100 gates. 100 planes going to 100 destinations.
- The moment you board one — 99 doors close. That's terrifying.
- So they walk around the boarding area. For years. Decades sometimes.
- They're not lazy. They're paralysed.

---

**SLIDE 7 — Failure to Constellate**
- Psychoanalyst term: a constellation is stars forming a picture
- The Puer has stars — lots of them, brilliant ones
- But they never connect. Never form a picture. Never become a life.
- *"A marginal life. Not a full life."*
- Close: *"Next video: what this actually looks like day to day — the cycle, the defenses, how it plays out in practice."*
