# Presenter Notes — Part 7: The Cure

---

**SLIDE 1 — Title**
- This is the final video in the series
- Recap the journey: we know what the Puer is, how they think, what keeps them stuck
- Today: the cure. And why it's so hard to hear.

---

**SLIDE 2 — Jung Was Asked: What Is the Cure?**
- Set this up as a moment — Jung, the greatest analyst of this pattern, was asked the question
- He paused. He thought. Then said one word: Work.
- Then he hesitated — *is it really as simple as that?*
- Yes. It is.
- Let that breathe. Don't rush past it.

---

**SLIDE 3 — Not Heroic Work**
- Immediately qualify — because the Puer will try to make it heroic
- Not a passionate 24-hour sprint. Not biohacking. Not finding the perfect system.
- Drab. Boring. Tuesday morning when you have to kick yourself into it.
- *"The thing they cannot do is the exact thing required"*
- This is why the cure sounds so unsatisfying — it's supposed to.

---

**SLIDE 4 — Focus on Cost, Not Gain**
- The Puer's default: always scanning for the upside, always eyes on the fantasy
- The shift: look at the cost. What does this actually require? What am I giving up? What will it feel like?
- *"Cannot pay cost without guarantee of gain — but the world doesn't work that way"*
- Every time you're considering something, ask: what's the cost? Can I pay it? Will I pay it consistently?

---

**SLIDE 5 — Sacrifice Potential in Chunks**
- This is the hardest one — it goes directly against the core fear
- Close doors. Deliberately. On purpose.
- Make a commitment knowing it limits other options — that's the point, not a side effect
- Building a life means giving up potential. That's what adulthood is.
- *"The stem cell has to differentiate. That's how it becomes something."*

---

**SLIDE 6 — Do the Small, Drab Things**
- Jordan Peterson's "clean your room" — same idea, from a Jungian tradition
- Life is not built on heroic moments. It's built on small things done consistently.
- Dishes. Laundry. Making the call. Showing up.
- *"Not as a philosophical act. Just because it needs doing."*
- No drama. No story. Just do it.

---

**SLIDE 7 — Warning: Don't Heroize the Cure**
- The complex will try to hijack this — watch for it
- *"I'm going to heroically embrace the ordinary"* — that's still the Puer talking
- If you make the drab life into a heroic project, you'll do it for a week and then stop
- The whole point is to do it without the story. Without the meaning. Just because it needs to be done.

---

**SLIDE 8 — The Irony**
- Slow down. This is the close.
- Everything they want — freedom, meaning, impact — is on the other side of commitment
- The entrepreneur dream requires dropping something. The relationship requires settling. The creative work requires years of boring craft.
- *"Walk through the door yourself — or get pushed through when you have no choice"*
- Every year in the boarding area: fewer doors available, same potential unused, no progress made
- Pause after this.

---

**SLIDE 9 — What It Means to Grow Up**
- Land the title. Bring it home.
- Doing the dishes. Laundry. Dishes again.
- *"That's it. That's what a life is made of."*
- And on the other side of that — a real one. A full one. A constellated one.
- Don't make it bigger than it needs to be. Let the simplicity do the work.
