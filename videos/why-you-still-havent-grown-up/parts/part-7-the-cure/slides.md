**SLIDE 1**
# Why You Still Haven't Grown Up
### Part 7: The Cure

---

**SLIDE 2**
## Jung Was Asked: What Is the Cure?
- He paused.
- He said one word.
- Then he hesitated — *is it really that simple?*
- **Work.**

---

**SLIDE 3**
## Not Heroic Work
- Not a 24-hour sprint of passion
- Not an optimised protocol
- Not the perfect circumstances
- Drab. Boring. Consistent. Ordinary.
- *The thing they cannot do is the exact thing required*

---

**SLIDE 4**
## Focus on Cost, Not Gain
- The Puer always scans for the upside — eyes on the fantasy
- Flip it: what does this actually cost?
- What am I signing up for?
- Cannot pay cost without guarantee of gain — but the world doesn't work that way

---

**SLIDE 5**
## Sacrifice Potential in Chunks
- Close doors deliberately
- Make commitments knowing they limit other options
- *That is the point*
- Building a life means giving up potential — that's what adulthood is

---

**SLIDE 6**
## Do the Small, Drab Things
- *"Clean your room — not as a philosophical act. Because it needs cleaning."*
- Dishes. Laundry. Show up Tuesday when nothing is exciting.
- Life is not built on 24-hour heroic bursts
- It is built on small, ordinary, consistent things — done without drama

---

**SLIDE 7**
## Warning: Don't Heroize the Cure
- The complex will try to hijack the solution
- *"I'm going to heroically embrace the drab"* — that's still the Puer
- You'll do it for a week. Then stop. Then games.
- Just do it. Without the story around it.

---

**SLIDE 8**
## The Irony
- The life you want is behind the door you won't walk through
- Commitment, settling, ordinary work — these are the path to the extraordinary outcome
- Every year in the boarding area: fewer doors, same potential, no progress
- *Walk through it yourself — or get pushed through when you have no choice*

---

**SLIDE 9**
## What It Means to Grow Up
- Doing the dishes
- Doing the laundry
- Doing the dishes again
- *That's it. That's what a life is made of.*
- And on the other side of that: a real one.
